Culture Clash

The Biological Weapon

Investigated by Timo Weiler
Nov 02, 2026

In Europe, prohibition signs usually make total sense. No smoking. No dogs. No trespassing. But travel to Vietnam, and you will suddenly find a new, highly specific icon next to the "No Smoking" sign: A crossed-out, spiky fruit.

This is the Durian. Some say it tastes like heaven. Absolutely everyone agrees it smells like a mixture of raw sewage, rotting onions, and despair. It is so pungent that it is legally banned from public transport, hotels, and elevators across Southeast Asia.

Design Working Perfectly

For once, this isn't a design fail. The icon is clear, bold, and absolutely necessary. If you ever ignore this sign and open a Durian indoors, you will instantly understand why this graphic design exists.
