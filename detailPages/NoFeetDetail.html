The Sink Incident

Investigated by Timo Weiler
Nov 01, 2026

There is a golden rule in the world of design and public management: A warning sign only exists because someone, somewhere, actually did the thing. The more specific the sign, the wilder the backstory.

This brings us to a public restroom offering a very specific instruction: Please do not wash your feet here. It is a sink. At waist height. This means that multiple people performed advanced gymnastics just to get their muddy toes into the basin.

A Failure of Architecture?

Honestly, if that many people feel the urge to wash their feet in your sink, maybe you didn't design a bad sign—maybe you forgot to build a footbath. Still, visualizing the acrobatics required to trigger this warning is hilarious.
