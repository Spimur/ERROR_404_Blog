Culture Clash

Nutritious Slop

Investigated by Timo Weiler
Oct 28, 2026

Starting your day with a healthy bowl of oats is a great idea, especially in the cold climate of Norway. But as a German-speaking tourist, buying your morning cereal suddenly feels like you are shopping at a farm supply store.

Behold: Hafer Frass. In Norwegian, this probably means something wonderful and healthy. But in German, "Fraß" translates to disgusting slop or animal feed. It is the exact word you use when the food is so bad it borders on a war crime.

Truth in Advertising?

Honestly, considering what some sugary cereals taste like, calling it "Oat Slop" might just be the most honest marketing campaign of the decade. We just hope it tastes better than it sounds.
