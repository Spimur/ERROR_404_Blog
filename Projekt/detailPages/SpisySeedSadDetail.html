Lost in Translation

Emotional Snacks

Investigated by Timo Weiler
Nov 05, 2026

When you visit a vibrant market in Thailand, you expect an explosion of flavors, exotic smells, and maybe a little bit of spicy food. What you do not expect is your snack to suffer from clinical depression.

According to the sign, this stall is selling "Spisy Seed Sad". Not only is the spelling beautifully creative, but the emotional state of the food raises a lot of questions. Why are the seeds sad? Is it because they are too spicy? Did the other snacks make fun of them?

Empathy Eating

We bought a bag just out of pity. We can confirm: they were incredibly spicy, but the sadness really brought out the flavor.
